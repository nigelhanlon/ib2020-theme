Coal Island


Just a speck to the human eye from the coast is a little island. Once believed to have been attached to the mainland, it remains stranded and isolated. There is little to see, a few trees but the beach has proven a great opportunity for photographs as the sands stand out being a dark gray to black tone rather than the traditional yellow.


Oxen Oil Rig


Drilling for oil has proven a lucrative endeavor and has provided finance for the Oxen Corporations in other areas of interest.


Black Smoker


A seabed hydrothermal vent appearing as black, chimney-like structures emitting hot clouds of black sulfides.


Coral Reef


A ridge of jagged rock, just below the surface of the sea, a delicate ecosystem lives tethering on the edge. Beautiful and unique fish and plant life, it's now at risk because of the oil drilling taking place off the coast.


Ship Graveyard


A strange section of the ocean where ships mysteriously disappear and apparently sink for no reason.


Choppy Waters


The vast expanse of water it goes on far into the horizon. There are rare periods of calm waters but Onda Ocean is known for its rough waters all year round.
